The Core Finding

Technology spend is now universal.
The performance gap is wider than ever.

Five Core Findings

What the research shows

Finding 01
Accidental Growth vs. Designed Motions
Most PE-backed companies reached their current ARR through growth that accumulated rather than was designed. When markets normalize, those companies struggle — they lack repeatable architecture. Top performers treat unfocused targeting as the root cause of pipeline failure, not rep quality.
Finding 02
The Rep Productivity Crisis is a System Failure
43% average AE quota attainment is not a talent problem. Reps spend only 28% of their week actually selling. High-performing organizations respond by moving toward engineered outbound — GTM Engineers automating administrative drag so human judgment concentrates at the conversion layer.
Finding 03
Centralized AI Governance Wins
Decentralized AI adoption — individual reps building their own GPTs — rarely reaches production quality. Companies winning the AI reset centralize it: a specialized team owns the customer journey and provides high-quality AI outputs as inputs for the sales team.
Finding 04
Annual Plans Fail by Q2
The traditional annual plan is increasingly obsolete. In its place: 90-day sprint models and habit-based demand generation. Effective demand gen is not a complex campaign — it is a consistent four-step loop: Create content → Ship to email list → Track signals → Call on signals.
Emerging Archetype
The Revenue Engineer
As these themes converge, a new professional archetype is emerging — the Revenue Engineer. This role combines GTM strategy with technical execution, replacing the traditional split between CROs and RevOps. For PE-backed companies, the most valuable skill is no longer mastery of a specific tool, but the adaptability to redesign the revenue system as new infrastructure emerges.
